Title: Scandium and zirconium additions in aluminum alloys
Source: literature summary for demo corpus

Demo excerpt:
Scandium additions in aluminum alloys are often discussed as a route to grain refinement,
dispersoid formation and strengthening. The same direction may carry a cost risk because
scandium is expensive compared with ordinary process-window changes.

Zirconium additions can support thermally stable dispersoids and may be explored as a partial
substitution route when the cost of scandium is too high for a target KPI contract.

Expected extracted claims:
- Scandium-containing dispersoids can refine grain structure and contribute to strengthening.
- Scandium additions are expensive and can violate a strict cost constraint.
- Zirconium may partially substitute expensive scandium additions in a cheaper route.

Title: Long-term aging of S-phase in aluminum alloy 2618A
Source: https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s10853-020-05740-x

Demo excerpt:
Aluminum alloy 2618A is used in elevated-temperature components. Long-term operation can
change hardening precipitates and degrade mechanical properties. A quantitative study of aging
at 190 C up to 25000 h investigated precipitate evolution, hardness and thermal behavior.

For the MVP, this document supports claims that aging temperature and aging time influence
precipitate state, and that precipitate state affects hardness and mechanical properties.

Expected extracted claims:
- Aging at elevated temperature changes the precipitate state and affects hardness.
- Long-term aging can coarsen S-phase precipitates.
- Coarsening is linked to degradation of mechanical properties.
